======================================================================
INTERPRETATION
======================================================================

The Baum-Welch algorithm is an EM (Expectation-Maximization) algorithm:

1. E-step: Compute γ and ξ (expected sufficient statistics)
   - γ_k(x): Expected time in state x at timestep k
   - ξ_k(x,x'): Expected transitions from x to x' at timestep k

2. M-step: Update parameters to maximize likelihood
   - π' = γ_1 (initial state distribution)
   - T'_{x,x'} = (expected x→x' transitions) / (expected time in x)
   - M'_{x,y} = (expected time in x with obs y) / (expected time in x)

After one iteration, the likelihood increased by 770.59%.
Multiple iterations would continue to improve the model until convergence.
